Maps appear to promise certainty. A thin line marks a road, a blue shape marks a lake, and a symbol marks a station. Yet every map is also a choice. It decides what to include, what to leave out, and what deserves attention. A map of a city might ignore birdsong, while a map of migration ignores traffic lights. Good readers of maps remember that they are guides rather than perfect copies of reality. The most useful map is not the one with the most information, but the one with the right information for the journey being made.
